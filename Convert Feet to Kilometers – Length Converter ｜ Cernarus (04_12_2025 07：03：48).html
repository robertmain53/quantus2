Methodology

This converter uses the international foot, which is defined as exactly 0.3048 meters in the International System of Units (SI). This definition is documented in the NIST Guide to the SI and associated conversion tables published by the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology.

The kilometer is the standard SI multiple for longer distances and is defined as exactly 1000 meters. Together, these definitions give a fixed and exact relationship between feet and kilometers.

To convert a distance from feet to kilometers, the tool first relates feet to meters using the exact factor 1 ft = 0.3048 m, and then converts meters to kilometers using 1 km = 1000 m.

Mathematically, the relationship can be written in two equivalent ways: kilometers = feet × 0.3048 ÷ 1000, or more compactly kilometers = feet ÷ 3280.84. Using the exact factor 0.3048, this means 1 foot corresponds to 0.0003048 kilometers.

Example step-by-step: suppose you have 5000 feet. First multiply by 0.3048 to convert to meters: 5000 × 0.3048 = 1524 meters. Then divide by 1000 to convert meters to kilometers: 1524 ÷ 1000 = 1.524 kilometers. The converter performs these steps instantly and shows the rounded output according to your display settings.

Because 0.3048 is exact, any rounding in your result comes only from numerical formatting or from how many significant figures you choose to display, not from the underlying conversion factor itself. For casual uses, three or four significant figures are usually enough; for engineering, surveying, or geospatial projects, you may keep additional digits and apply the rounding rules required by your standard or regulation.

Historically, some U.S. surveying work used the so-called U.S. survey foot, which differs very slightly from the international foot. Modern NIST and federal guidance has transitioned to the international foot for consistency with SI, and the difference is negligible for everyday and most professional uses over modest distances.

Worked examples

Quick reference values: 1 ft = 0.0003048 km, 10 ft = 0.003048 km, 100 ft = 0.03048 km, 1000 ft = 0.3048 km. These values are useful as anchors for mental estimation.

Everyday walking distance: 500 ft × 0.0003048 = 0.1524 km. This might correspond to a short walk across a parking lot or between nearby buildings on a campus.

Running and fitness: A 10,000 ft route is 10,000 × 0.0003048 = 3.048 km, helpful for converting a workout logged in feet or yards to a training plan written in kilometers.

Human height context: Someone who is 6 ft tall has a height of 6 × 0.0003048 = 0.0018288 km, or about 1.83 meters. While kilometers are not normally used for body height, this emphasizes how small a foot is compared with a kilometer.

Geographic and aviation distances: A jet cruising at 32,500 ft is at 32,500 × 0.0003048 ≈ 9.906 km. This type of conversion appears in physics, atmospheric science, and aviation training materials.

Consistency check with miles: Since 1 mile is 5280 ft and 1 mile is approximately 1.609 km, you can check the factor by computing 5280 × 0.0003048 ≈ 1.609 km. This confirms that the foot-to-kilometer relationship is consistent with standard mile-to-kilometer conversions.

Expert Q&A

What is the formula to convert feet to kilometers?

The core formula is kilometers = feet × 0.0003048. This comes from the exact definition 1 foot = 0.3048 meters and 1 kilometer = 1000 meters, so kilometers = feet × 0.3048 ÷ 1000.

How many kilometers are in one foot?

One foot corresponds to 0.0003048 kilometers. Because this factor is based on the exact SI definition of the foot, it does not change over time or across sources.

How can I convert feet to kilometers in my head?

A useful mental shortcut is to divide feet by about 3300. Since the exact relationship is kilometers = feet ÷ 3280.84, dividing by 3300 gives a close approximation that is usually accurate enough for rough estimates. For example, 3300 ft ÷ 3300 ≈ 1 km, and 6600 ft ÷ 3300 ≈ 2 km.

How many steps are there in a kilometer?

The answer depends on your stride length. A typical adult walking stride might be about 2.5 ft to 2.7 ft per step, which is around 0.76 m to 0.82 m. Since 1 km is 3280.84 ft, a kilometer is on the order of 1200 to 1300 steps for many adults. Fitness trackers and gait analyses can refine this estimate for your specific stride.

Why are feet still used if kilometers are the SI standard?

Feet remain common in fields such as construction, real estate, and aviation in countries that historically adopted imperial or U.S. customary units. However, scientific publications, engineering standards, and international regulations increasingly prefer SI units like meters and kilometers to reduce confusion and support global interoperability.

Which definition of the foot does this calculator use?

This calculator uses the international foot, defined as exactly 0.3048 meters in SI terms. This is the definition recommended by NIST and adopted in recent U.S. federal guidance for most applications. The older U.S. survey foot is no longer recommended except in legacy surveying contexts.

Is this converter accurate enough for engineering or geospatial work?

Yes. The conversion factor itself is exact, and any small differences in final values come from how many digits you choose to keep or how you round. For high-stakes engineering, surveying, or GIS projects, you should carry enough significant figures internally, follow your governing standard or regulation, and document whether you used the international foot or a legacy definition.

Can I use this tool for aviation altitudes reported in feet?

You can use this converter to express aviation altitudes in kilometers for educational, analytical, or reporting purposes. Operational aviation procedures, however, are usually defined in feet or flight levels, so for safety you should always follow the units used in official documents, checklists, and air traffic control instructions.

Can I convert kilometers back to feet with this tool?

This specific tool is designed for feet to kilometers. The inverse relationship is feet = kilometers × 1000 ÷ 0.3048. In practice, it is often more convenient to use a dedicated kilometers-to-feet converter or a bidirectional tool that lets you switch the input and output units with a single control.

Why do scientific and engineering references recommend SI units like meters and kilometers?

SI units provide a globally consistent system for length, mass, time, and other quantities. Using meters and kilometers simplifies communication across disciplines and countries, reduces the risk of unit mix-ups, and aligns with guidance from national metrology institutes and university physics and engineering programs.
